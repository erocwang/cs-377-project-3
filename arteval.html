This is a research project being conducted by students in CS377: Human Work in Computational Systems as part of a course project. The faculty advisor is Molly Feldman, a member of the Department of Computer Science at Williams College.

What Will You Be Asked to Do?
If you decide to participate, you will be asked to consecutively rate and give your reasoning for your rating for a work of art on a scale from 1-10. It should take you about 2 minutes to complete.

Risks
Except for your time and inconvenience, there are no risks to you from participating in this study. The risks are equivalent to using the internet.

Benefits As a participant, you will be monetarily compensated for your time and effort. In addition, you may have some enjoyment from viewing works of art and considering their value. Your input may help contribute to research in evaluating if and how the crowd can quantify subjective judgements.

Compensation
You will receive $0.25 for completing this HIT.

Confidentiality
This study is anonymous. Please do not write your name or any identifiable information in your response. Data will be initially stored on Amazon as per their privacy policy. Your input will be anonymized once removed from MTurk and be stored locally for around 30 days.

Conditions for Rejection
Participation is voluntary. If you choose to take part in this study, you may stop at any time. However, you must complete at least 1 HIT to be eligible for compensation. In addition, lack of reasoning in your evaluation explanations can be means for rejection.

If You Have Questions
If you have any further questions, you may contact Molly Feldman at mqf1@williams.edu.

Statement of Consent
By accepting and completing this HIT, you acknowledge that you have read the above information and consent to take part in this study.
